5. Regulatory Status — MSB Exemption and Reasons of non-regulatory status.

This Section sets forth the factual, legal, and structural basis upon which A2A Global asserts that it is not a "money transmitter" or "money services business" under federal or state law. These provisions are incorporated into these Terms as binding contractual acknowledgments by all Users and establish the evidentiary framework supporting A2A Global's regulatory position.

5.1. Reason 1 — Integral-to-Sale-of-Goods-or-Services Exemption (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(F))

Under 31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(F), a person that "accepts and transmits funds only integral to the sale of goods or the provision of services, other than money transmission services, by the person who is accepting and transmitting the funds" is not a money transmitter.

A2A Global's Platform coordinates the flow of funds between Payers and Receivers exclusively in connection with bona fide marketplace transactions for professional services. Any acceptance, holding, or transmission of funds — which is performed solely by Licensed Service Providers, not by A2A Global — is integral to, and inseparable from, the provision of marketplace services (project management, milestone tracking, contract management, quality assurance, and dispute resolution) that constitute A2A Global's primary business activity. The fund coordination component is not a separate and discrete service provided in addition to the marketplace services; it is a necessary and integral part of those services. This analysis is consistent with FinCEN Administrative Rulings FIN-2014-R004 (escrow services in internet sales) and FIN-2014-R005 (secured transaction management services), in which FinCEN determined that companies providing escrow and transaction management in connection with internet sales of goods and services were not money transmitters because the acceptance and transmission of funds was only integral to the provision of those services.

5.2. Reason 2 — Technology and Network Access Provider Exemption (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(A))

Under 31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(A), a person that "provides the delivery, communication, or network access services used by a money transmitter to support money transmission services" is not a money transmitter.

A2A Global develops, maintains, and operates proprietary software — a technology platform — that provides communication, network access, data exchange, API connectivity, and workflow tools used by Licensed Service Providers to deliver regulated financial services. A2A Global's role is analogous to that of a technology infrastructure provider: it builds and operates the software through which Licensed Service Providers connect with Payers and Receivers. The Platform is the delivery, communication, and network access layer; the Licensed Service Provider is the regulated entity performing money transmission (if any). This is consistent with FinCEN Administrative Ruling FIN-2014-R007, in which FinCEN confirmed that a company providing technology infrastructure does not become a money transmitter by virtue of that infrastructure being used in connection with financial transactions.

5.3. Reason 3 — No Custody, No Control, No Possession of Funds (Substance-Over-Form)

FinCEN applies a "substance-over-form" test to determine whether a person is a money transmitter. The test examines whether the person's fundamental business activity is money transmission or something else, regardless of labels or form (FinCEN Interpretive Guidance FIN-2019-G001).

A2A Global's fundamental business activity is the operation of a marketplace and software platform that connects Payers and Receivers. A2A Global:

Never accepts currency, funds, or value from any person for transmission to another person or location.

Never holds, possesses, custodies, or controls User funds at any time.

Never transmits currency, funds, or value to any person or location.

Does not have signatory authority, withdrawal rights, or unilateral control over the Platform Account. The Platform Account is held with and operated by the Licensed Service Provider.

Cannot initiate, freeze, reverse, or redirect fund transfers without action by the Licensed Service Provider.

Earns revenue exclusively from Platform Fees charged for technology and marketplace services, not from spreads, float, exchange rates, or any other income derived from the movement or custody of funds.

Under the substance-over-form analysis, A2A Global's fundamental activity is marketplace operation and software development — not money transmission. The incidental coordination of fund flows through Licensed Service Providers is ancillary to the primary marketplace services.

5.4. Reason 4 — Licensed Service Provider Delegation Model

All regulated financial activities — including customer onboarding, identity verification, KYC/KYB screening, sanctions screening, fund acceptance, fund custody, escrow, processing, settlement, disbursement, and regulatory reporting — are performed exclusively by Licensed Service Providers under their own state and federal licenses and regulatory obligations. A2A Global has entered into formal agreements with Licensed Service Providers under which:

The Licensed Service Provider accepts, holds, and transmits funds in its own name, under its own license, and subject to its own BSA/AML compliance program.

The Licensed Service Provider operates the Platform Account and all Provider Accounts.

The Licensed Service Provider performs all KYC/KYB, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and other regulatory obligations independently.

The Licensed Service Provider deducts the Platform Fee on behalf of A2A Global before disbursing remaining funds to the Receiver.

A2A Global does not substitute for, circumvent, or perform any regulated function that the Licensed Service Provider is required to perform.

This structure is analogous to the Upwork model, in which Upwork Inc operates the marketplace and a separate licensed entity (Upwork Escrow Inc, a licensed Internet escrow company) handles all escrow and fund settlement functions under its own license.

5.5. Reason 5 — Payment Processor Exemption Support (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(B))

Under 31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(B), a person that "acts as a payment processor to facilitate the purchase of, or payment of a bill for, a good or service through a clearance and settlement system by agreement with the creditor or seller" is not a money transmitter. While A2A Global itself does not process payments (the Licensed Service Provider does), this exemption provides additional structural support because:

All fund flows through the Platform facilitate the purchase of professional services — not money transmission itself.

All fund flows are processed through clearance and settlement systems that admit only BSA-regulated financial institutions (e.g., ACH, card networks operated by BSA-regulated institutions).

The Licensed Service Provider operates pursuant to formal written agreements with A2A Global and with Receivers.

Funds are transmitted only to the Receiver (the creditor/seller of services) and to A2A Global (as Platform Fee for marketplace services rendered) — not to any other location or person.

FinCEN has confirmed these four conditions in multiple administrative rulings (FIN-2014-R012, FIN-2013-R002) and in the 2019 CVC Guidance (FIN-2019-G001).

5.6. Reason 6 — Agent-of-Payee Structural Protection (State-Level)

In approximately 22 U.S. states, the "agent of the payee" exemption provides that a person who receives money on behalf of a payee (the party owed payment) under a preexisting written contract is not engaged in money transmission, because delivery of funds to the agent satisfies the payor's obligation to the payee.

The Licensed Service Provider contracted by A2A Global acts as the agent of the Receiver (payee) in accepting payment from the Payer (payor). Under the formal agreements between the Licensed Service Provider and each Receiver, the Licensed Service Provider is appointed as the Receiver's agent for purposes of receiving payment, and the Payer's payment to the Licensed Service Provider satisfies the Payer's obligation to the Receiver. A2A Global does not itself function as an agent of the payee; rather, the Licensed Service Provider does, providing a separate layer of regulatory insulation for A2A Global.

5.7. Reason 7 — Transaction Management Services Precedent (FIN-2014-R004 / FIN-2014-R005)

FinCEN's Administrative Rulings FIN-2014-R004 and FIN-2014-R005 established that internet-based companies providing escrow services and secured transaction management services in connection with the sale of goods and services are not money transmitters where the acceptance and transmission of funds: (a) is not a separate and discrete service provided in addition to the underlying marketplace/transaction management service; (b) is a necessary and integral part of the service itself; and (c) the company provides active transaction management including establishing conditions precedent, verifying performance, validating delivery, and managing disputes.

A2A Global's Platform satisfies all three criteria:

Fund coordination is not a standalone service — it is inseparable from the marketplace and project management functionality of the Platform.

Fund coordination is necessary and integral — without it, the Platform's milestone-based project management, quality assurance, and dispute resolution features could not function.

A2A Global provides active transaction management — the Platform establishes Service Contracts with defined milestones and conditions precedent, verifies deliverable submission, validates acceptance by the Payer, and manages disputes, before funds are released by the Licensed Service Provider.

By contrast, FinCEN ruled in FIN-2014-R006 that a company that merely operated a payment platform without providing transaction management, verification, or validation was a money transmitter. A2A Global is clearly distinguishable from the FIN-2014-R006 scenario because A2A Global provides substantial marketplace and transaction management services beyond mere fund transfer.

5.8. Reason 8 — Primary Business Activity Test

FinCEN's regulations provide that whether a person is a money transmitter is "a matter of facts and circumstances" (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)). FinCEN evaluates what constitutes the person's "primary business activity." Where the primary business activity is something other than money transmission, and any fund movement is ancillary to that primary activity, the person is not a money transmitter.

A2A Global's primary business activity — by revenue model, organizational structure, technology investment, personnel allocation, marketing, and operational focus — is the operation of a marketplace and software platform. Specifically:

Revenue Model: A2A Global earns Platform Fees for marketplace and technology services. It does not earn revenue from spreads, float, exchange rates, interchange, or any other income derived from the movement or custody of funds.

Technology Investment: The substantial majority of A2A Global's capital and personnel is devoted to software development, platform operations, matching algorithms, project management tools, and user experience — not to financial operations.

Operational Focus: A2A Global's day-to-day operations involve marketplace curation, user support, quality assurance, and platform improvement — not fund processing, settlement, or regulatory compliance (which is handled by Licensed Service Providers).

Organizational Structure: A2A Global does not employ compliance officers, BSA officers, or money transmission operations staff because it does not engage in money transmission. Licensed Service Providers maintain their own compliance infrastructure.

5.9. Reason 9 — No Separate Money Transmission Service

Under FinCEN's regulatory framework, money transmission requires the "acceptance" of funds from one person "and" the "transmission" of those funds to another person or location (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(i)(A)). Both elements — acceptance AND transmission — must be present and must constitute a distinct, separately identifiable service.

A2A Global does not accept funds from any person. A2A Global does not transmit funds to any person. The Licensed Service Provider — a separate, independently licensed entity — performs both acceptance and transmission. A2A Global's Platform merely coordinates the informational and workflow elements of the transaction (project posting, matching, contracting, milestone management, deliverable verification, and dispute resolution). No User can use the Platform to send money to another User independent of a bona fide Service Contract for professional services. The Platform does not offer, and cannot be used for, standalone fund transfers, person-to-person payments, remittances, or any transaction that is not integral to the provision of marketplace services.

5.10. Reason 10 — Federal and State Regulatory Safe Harbor Architecture

A2A Global's regulatory architecture is designed to operate within multiple, overlapping federal and state exemptions and safe harbors:

Federal — BSA/FinCEN: A2A Global relies on the Integral-to-Sale Exemption (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(F)), the Technology/Network Access Provider Exemption (31 CFR § 1010.100(ff)(5)(ii)(A)), and the substance-over-form analysis confirming that A2A Global's fundamental activity is marketplace operation, not money transmission.

Federal — 18 U.S.C. § 1960: A2A Global does not operate an "unlicensed money transmitting business" because (a) A2A Global is not a money transmitting business at all under the analysis above, and (b) all regulated financial activity is performed by separately licensed and registered Licensed Service Providers.

State Money Transmission Laws: A2A Global does not directly accept, hold, or transmit funds in any state and is further insulated by the agent-of-payee structures maintained by Licensed Service Providers in states that recognize such exemptions. In states that require money transmitter licenses, the Licensed Service Provider — not A2A Global — holds the applicable license.

CFPB / Regulation E: To the extent any transaction through the Platform constitutes an "electronic fund transfer" under Regulation E, the Licensed Service Provider — not A2A Global — is the "financial institution" responsible for compliance.

This multi-layered safe harbor architecture ensures that A2A Global operates within the boundaries of applicable law regardless of which specific exemption or defense is invoked, and that no single point of regulatory failure can result in A2A Global being classified as a money transmitter.
